Cracks, Sinking Floors & Water Problems — Fixed at the Source

Homes across Jacksonville deal with settling foundations, moisture-damaged crawl spaces, and water that shows up where it shouldn't. Settled driveways and sidewalks from the same soil movement can be fixed with concrete leveling — no tear-out needed. Here's how we handle the rest.

Foundation Repair

Cracks spreading across your walls, doors that won't close, floors sloping toward one side of the house — these are signs your foundation is settling. Jacksonville's sandy soil shifts and compresses unevenly, especially in areas built on filled land. We install steel push piers or helical piers driven deep past the unstable soil to stabilize and lift your home back toward level.

Learn More
Basement Waterproofing

Water seeping through your basement walls or rising through floor cracks after a heavy rain means water is finding a path your home can't handle. We install an interior drainage system along the perimeter that intercepts water before it reaches your living space and routes it to a sump pump that handles the rest.

Learn More
Crawl Space Repair

Floors that bounce, sag, or feel soft underfoot usually mean the supports beneath your crawl space are failing. Wood posts rot, block columns lean, and floor joists weaken — especially in older Jacksonville homes where ground moisture has been working on them for decades. We replace failing supports with adjustable steel jack posts and sister or replace damaged joists to make your floors solid again.

Learn More
Crawl Space Encapsulation

A musty smell drifting up from below your floors, mold growing on crawl space joists, or humidity that never drops inside your home — all of it starts with an open, damp crawl space. We seal the crawl space with a heavy-duty vapor barrier, close the vents, and install a commercial dehumidifier that keeps humidity below the mold threshold year-round.

Learn More
Sump Pump Installation

If your basement or crawl space takes on water during storms, a properly installed sump pump is your last line of defense. We install a submersible pump in a lined basin with a battery backup system so water gets removed automatically — even during a power outage in the middle of hurricane season.

Learn More
French Drain Installation

Water pooling against your foundation wall or collecting under your floors needs somewhere to go. A French drain gives it a clear path — perforated pipe set in gravel captures groundwater and routes it to a sump pump or discharge point before it pushes against your foundation.

Diagonal cracks radiating from door and window corners are one of the clearest signs of foundation settlement. In Jacksonville, the sandy soil beneath your home can shift unevenly — especially if your lot was built on filled land or near the water table. As one section of the foundation settles faster than the rest, the structure flexes and cracks appear at the weakest points: corners of openings. These cracks tend to get wider over time, not smaller.

Some initial settling is normal in the first few years after construction. But if you're seeing new cracks, sticking doors, or sloping floors in a home that's been standing for years, that's active settlement — not old settling that stopped. The difference matters because active settlement means the soil is still moving and the problem will continue to get worse. A free inspection with actual measurements is the fastest way to know which one you're dealing with.

Standard homeowner's insurance doesn't cover foundation damage caused by soil movement, settling, or poor drainage — which are the causes behind most foundation problems in Jacksonville. Some policies cover sudden events like a burst pipe that washes out soil beneath the foundation, but normal settlement and moisture-related damage are excluded. It's worth checking your policy, but plan on this being an out-of-pocket repair.
