What We Fix in Tulsa Homes

Tulsa's red clay soil swells when it rains and shrinks when it dries. That back-and-forth is hard on every foundation in this area. Here's how we help.

Foundation Repair

Doors that won't close right. Cracks above your windows. Floors that lean to one side. That's the clay pulling away from your foundation during dry spells, then pushing back when it rains. We put piers deep enough that the clay can't mess with your foundation anymore.

Basement Waterproofing

Water showing up on your basement floor after a storm isn't something you just mop up and forget. It means water is finding a way through your walls or floor joints. We stop it with drainage and sealing so your basement stays dry — even during a heavy Oklahoma spring.

Learn More
Crawl Space Repair

If your floors feel bouncy or soft when you walk across them, something underneath has given out. In older Tulsa homes — Brookside, Midtown, Florence Park — the beams and joists in the crawl space take years of moisture damage before anyone notices. We get down there, replace what's rotted, and put in steel supports to level things out.

Learn More
Crawl Space Encapsulation

That musty smell in your house? It's coming from your crawl space. Oklahoma humidity sits high all year, and that moisture feeds mold and rots the wood holding up your floors. We seal the whole space with a heavy vapor barrier and dehumidifier so the moisture stays out for good.

Learn More
Sump Pump Installation

Some Tulsa homes — especially near Mingo Creek, Bird Creek, or low spots along the Arkansas River — get water in the crawl space or basement every time it rains hard. A sump pump catches that water before it sits and causes damage.

Learn More
French Drain Installation

Clay soil doesn't drain well. Water pools against your foundation instead of running away from it. Over time, that pressure cracks walls and pushes moisture inside. A french drain moves the water away from the house before it becomes a problem.

Foundation Repair Questions From Tulsa Homeowners

Real questions we hear from homeowners in this area.

The red clay soil here swells when it gets wet and shrinks when it dries out. Tulsa gets heavy rain in spring then bakes through summer — that cycle moves the ground under your house every year. Over time, the shifting cracks walls, sticks doors, and settles one side of the foundation lower than the other.

Diagonal cracks from window or door corners, stair-step cracks in brick, and horizontal cracks along a wall usually mean the foundation has moved. If doors are sticking or floors are sloping at the same time, it's worth getting a free inspection. A hairline vertical crack by itself is usually just cosmetic.

It gets worse. The clay keeps moving every season, so cracks widen, doors jam harder, and water finds new ways in. Plumbing lines under a slab can crack from the shifting too. A smaller job now beats a bigger job later.

It's a moisture problem, and it can turn into a structural one. Oklahoma humidity feeds mold and rots the wood beams in your crawl space. Encapsulation seals it out. If the wood is already damaged, the structural supports need to be fixed first.

For settling foundations, steel piers or helical piers go deep past the clay to soil that doesn't move. For cracks in basement or foundation walls, basement wall repair with carbon fiber or wall anchors stops the bowing. For sinking driveways and sidewalks, concrete leveling lifts the slab back into place. If you want to add light or a safe exit to a basement, egress window installation can be done at the same time. We match the method to your home — there's no one-size-fits-all here.
