Foundation & Crawl Space Solutions for Asheville Homes

Mountain terrain, heavy rainfall, and clay-rich soils create real problems for homes across Buncombe County. Here's how we fix them.

Foundation Repair

If your home has diagonal cracks from window corners, sticking doors, or uneven floors, your foundation may be settling. Asheville's hillside lots and clay-rich saprolite soil are common culprits — fill dirt placed during original construction compresses over time, and one section of the home sinks while the rest stays put. We stabilize settling foundations with push piers and helical pier installation — driven past the unstable soil until they hit solid bearing ground — and lock your home back in place. Learn more about foundation repair.

Basement Waterproofing

Water getting into your basement isn't just annoying — it's a sign that pressure is building in the soil outside your walls. Whether it's seeping through floor cracks after a storm or draining through wall cracks during heavy rain, the goal is the same: intercept it before it spreads. We install interior drainage systems, perimeter drain tile, and sump pumps to capture water at the source and pump it away automatically. Learn more about basement waterproofing.

Learn More
Crawl Space Repair

Moisture rots wood supports, floor joists, and beams underneath your home. When floors start sagging or feeling bouncy, the structure below needs attention. We replace failing supports with steel jack posts and repair or sister damaged joists.

Learn More
Crawl Space Encapsulation

Asheville's mountain humidity doesn't stay outside — it works its way into every unprotected crawl space. Ground moisture evaporates from exposed dirt, condenses on wood framing, and creates the damp, musty conditions that mold and rot need to thrive. Crawl space encapsulation seals the dirt floor and walls with a heavy-duty 20-mil liner, controls humidity with a commercial dehumidifier, and turns a damp problem space into a clean, dry one. Learn more about crawl space encapsulation.

Learn More
Sump Pump Installation

A sump pump is the last line of defense between your basement and groundwater — but only when it's working. In Asheville, heavy rain events push water up fast, and a pump that hesitates or fails during a storm can mean serious damage within hours. We install cast iron submersible pumps built for continuous duty, plus battery backup systems that take over automatically if power goes out during a storm. Learn more about sump pump installation.

Learn More
French Drain Installation

On Asheville's sloped lots, rainwater has to go somewhere. When it doesn't have a clear path, it runs toward the foundation. A properly installed French drain gives it a better option — capturing water in a perforated pipe bedded in gravel and routing it away from your home before it ever reaches the walls or the crawl space. Learn more about French drain installation.

Hairline cracks that stay the same size for years are usually cosmetic. Cracks that are diagonal, wider than a quarter inch, or growing over time typically mean the foundation is moving. Sticking doors and windows, uneven floors, and cracks that appear above door frames are signs the movement is structural. If you're not sure, a free inspection gives you a clear answer — and if it turns out to be cosmetic, we'll tell you that too.

That musty smell is moisture evaporating from exposed dirt in the crawl space. In Asheville's climate, ground moisture is constant — and 40-50% of the air you breathe on your first floor comes up from below. That moisture feeds mold on floor joists and beams, causes wood rot over time, and raises humidity levels throughout the house. Crawl space encapsulation seals the source and a dehumidifier keeps it controlled year-round.

Foundation and crawl space problems are progressive — they always get worse, never better. A crawl space that's damp today will have mold in a few months and rotting joists within a couple of years.
